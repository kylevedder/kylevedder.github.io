I'm Kyle Vedder

I am a PhD student under Eric Eaton in the Lifelong Machine Learning Lab in GRASP at the University of Pennsylvania. My work focuses on continual learning in service robots, building towards care robots for the elderly and disabled.

In undergrad I worked under Joydeep Biswas in the Autonomous Mobile Robotics Lab at UMass Amherst on the RoboCup SSL team UMass Minutebots where I worked on the core infrastructure, the low level safety system, and did research in single-agent and multi-agent path finding.

I have prior industry experience as an intern at Google, Amazon Lab126, and an aquired startup.

Feel free to email me about my research, my work, or for advice about things like being a CS undergrad or applying to grad school.
